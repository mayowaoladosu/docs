## Observability surface

The Observability area in Nubis brings together platform health, resource metrics, logs, incident history, and alerting workflows so operators can move from detection to action quickly.

## What you can monitor

- **Platform status** for global health and incident visibility.
- **Compute and project metrics** such as utilization, throughput, and resource performance over time.
- **Resource logs** for workloads like virtual machines and managed databases.
- **Alert policies and delivery history** so teams can see what fired and where notifications were sent.

## Alerting

Nubis supports alert destinations, alert policies, and event history at the organization level. Use alerting when you want an operator-facing signal instead of waiting for a dashboard check.

Typical patterns include:

- CPU or memory thresholds on critical workloads.
- Project-level spikes in usage or error rate.
- Follow-up actions after incident creation or degraded platform status.

## Recommended workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check platform status first">
    If multiple resources look unhealthy at once, confirm whether the issue is platform-wide before debugging a single workload.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inspect metrics and logs together">
    Use metrics to identify when a problem started and logs to understand what changed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn repeat incidents into alerts">
    Once a failure pattern is clear, promote it into an alert policy so the team is notified earlier next time.
  </Step>
</Steps>
