## Onboarding

Nubis is built around a simple operating model: your team works inside an organization, that organization owns one or more projects, and each project contains the resources you run in production.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Sign in to the Nubis Console and complete your initial account profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create or join an organization">
    Organizations are the top-level boundary for billing, membership, support plans, and shared governance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your first project">
    Projects scope workloads, networking, billing visibility, and API activity. Most teams create one project per product or environment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add billing and verification">
    Attach a payment method, complete any required billing or phone verification, and decide on spend controls before launching production resources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite your team">
    Use [roles and permissions](/administration/rbac/overview) to give each teammate the right access level from day one.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Launch your first resources

Once billing is enabled, you can start with any of these:

- [Compute Instances](/services/instances/create) for app servers, workers, and general-purpose workloads.
- [Managed Databases](/console/databases) for stateful services in the same control plane.
- [Storage](/console/storage) for block volumes, buckets, and access keys.
- [Networking](/console/networking) for VPC-style networking, firewalls, load balancers, DNS, and domains.
- [Kubernetes](/console/kubernetes) when you want orchestrated clusters instead of single instances.
