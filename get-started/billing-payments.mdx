## Billing readiness matters early

Billing is not just a finance setup task. It controls whether teams can launch resources confidently, whether usage is visible in time, and whether unexpected spend is caught before it becomes painful.

## What to do before launch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a payment method">
    Connect the billing provider available for your organization and confirm the default collection method.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clear any verification gates">
    Complete phone, billing, or organization checks that may block provisioning or payment collection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review usage visibility">
    Make sure your team knows where usage summaries, breakdowns, invoices, and credit balances are managed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set spend controls">
    Apply organization-level or project-level limits before multiple teams begin launching resources.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What billing should answer for your team

- Which organization pays for a workload
- Which project is driving cost
- Which services are growing fastest
- Which invoices are open or recently collected
- Whether credits or vouchers are being used correctly

## Good billing habits

- Enable billing before migration or production cutover windows.
- Review usage weekly during early adoption.
- Put finance and platform stakeholders in the same billing review loop.
- Treat spend controls as part of setup, not cleanup.
